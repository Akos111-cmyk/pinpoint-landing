FAQ

Common questions, straight answers

From first conversation to deployed solution — what to expect at every stage.

A prioritised roadmap of automation opportunities, each with an estimated implementation cost, projected annual savings, and payback period. You leave with a concrete plan, not a generic slide deck. Every recommendation is specific to your business and backed by data from your own operations.
It follows a structured process. First, interviews with the people doing the work — not just management — to surface the real bottlenecks. Then the current workflow is mapped and analysed. Findings cover three types of opportunity: cost reduction (time saved, headcount freed), revenue growth (faster lead response, better conversion, upsell triggers), and quality improvement (fewer errors, more consistent outputs). Everything is ranked by impact and effort, and delivered as a phased executive roadmap.
The audit starts with a 1–2 hour department workshop, followed by short interviews with 5–10 people from the relevant team. After that, the analytical work happens independently — no ongoing input needed from your side. Quick wins are typically live within 2–4 weeks of the implementation phase starting. During build, a project manager handles all coordination so your team is involved only when it matters.
Both. The full engagement covers build and deployment. Solutions are custom-built, tested, and handed over running — not delivered as a specification for your IT team to figure out. Post-launch, a separate support and maintenance service is available to keep everything running as your business evolves.
The audit deliverable stands on its own — you can take the roadmap and execute it with your own team or another partner. There's no obligation to proceed with implementation. Many clients use the audit to get internal buy-in before committing to a build phase.
